About Me

I am an undergraduate student at the University of Bath pursuing an integrated, interdisciplinary masters in Electrical and Mechanical Engineering with a concentration in robotics and autonomous technologies. I designed this website to publicly showcase my many interests, professional experiences, and most recent coding projects, and to teach myself web development in the process.

At the University of Bath, I am a student researcher in the AI division of the United Kingdom’s leading Formula Student team, Team Bath Racing Electric. Over the past year, I have been working on iteratively developing the perception pipeline for the team’s autonomous racing car, which will hopefully be deployed for the 2021 FS-AI competition. More information on this project can be found here.

As a result of my father’s background in radiology, I became enthralled by the augmentative capabilities of artificial intelligence within the area of healthcare. Currently, I am working on an extensive research project to develop a deep learning model for the early diagnosis of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease. Additionally, stemming from a passion for the arts and entertainment, I hope to learn more about the creative potential of computer science technologies, particularly how artificial intelligence and machine learning can be used to enrich creative processes such as visual storytelling and music production.

When I’m not advancing my knowledge of computer science and artificial intelligence through personal and academic projects, such as those listed here, I divide my time between my various hobbies and interests. This includes anything from playing tactical shooters like VALORANT while listening to Taylor Swift to watching my favorite films and television shows. I am also currently learning how to play the piano through online lessons.

In 2016, I was one of ten annual recipients of the Crown Prince’s International Scholarship, a merit-based scholarship that provides me with the opportunity to study up to the doctoral level with no commitments. I plan to use this opportunity to collaborate with and learn from a diverse network of scholars and academics, thus developing my computer science skills and preparing myself for a lucrative career in technology.

This project is still in progress and is the first stage of a larger project in collaboration with the Central Scientific Instruments Organisation. The primary focus is to develop machine learning models to automatically detect and track endangered animals in thermal images for conservation purposes. More details will be provided once the code is completed.

TBReAI21 Perception

Worked on research and development of a deep learning model for the detection and localization of cones for autonomous racing. The current implementation is built upon the YOLOv3-Tiny network and utilizes multiple sensory inputs from stereo cameras and 3D LiDAR to evaluate features of the environment. Future work includes improving the computational efficiency of the system and and integrating it into Team Bath Racing Electric’s autonomous racing car for the FS-AI competition.

SpikeSort

In recent years, neuroscience research has looked into the activity of individual neurons in extracellular recordings for brain-machine interfaces and the treatment of memory loss and paralysis. For this project, a computational intelligence approach was used to create a system that processes these recordings, which contain thousands of neural spikes, and automatically sorts the extracted spikes into classes associated with the neuron that produced it. Simulated annealing was used during cross-validation for parameter optimization.

Medical Image Enhancement

Medical images, such as MRI, ultrasound, and CT scan images, are generally characterized by multiplicative speckle noise that reduces the image contrast and obscures fine details. Several fundamental filters were implemented in MATLAB to reduce the noise in these images while preserving the boundaries. Their performances were evaluated and compared through the use of the Canny edge detector.

Details

Swiftalysis

Collected data by scraping the web for Taylor Swift’s lyrics using the Genius API and BeautifulSoup. Extracted topics from the lyrics using non-negative matrix factorization and performed an unsupervised analysis of the sentiments evoked by each of her albums. Additionally, using the collected data, both character-level and word-level text generators were built using recurrent neural networks in an attempt to generate lyrics similar to those written by Taylor Swift.

Path Planning for Autonomous Vehicles

Designed and developed the navigation subsystem for an autonomous wheelchair, which consists of a global planner (modified A* search algorithm) and a local planner (dynamic window approach). Project includes a Python module that simulates path planning and navigation in known and partially known 2D environments.

Variable-Speed CPU Fan Controller

Designed an embedded system in C to control the speed of a 3-pin CPU fan. The speed can be modified manually through linear voltage regulation by turning a rotary knob, or automatically through thermostatic control. An intuitive user interface was created using the switches, seven-segment displays, LEDs, and buttons on an Altera DE1-SoC FPGA.

iOS app that offers route planning for electrical vehicles (EV) based on the state of charge of the vehicle’s battery and the locations of the nearest EV charging stations. Included features an interactive map with compatible charging stations, detailed analytics of the state of the user’s vehicle, display customization, among others.
